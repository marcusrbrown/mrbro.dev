# Product

## Register

brand

## Platform

web

## Users

The primary audience is open-source peers evaluating Marcus's work and deciding what to explore.

## Product Purpose

The site exists to establish open-source credibility. Success means a visitor explores the shipped projects; the blog is the fallback path for anyone not ready to dig into code yet.

## Positioning

Engineering with taste — technical depth and design judgment proven through maintained work, not claimed in prose.

## Conversion & proof

- Primary and secondary CTA: Explore projects (primary); Read the blog (secondary)
- The line a visitor remembers after 10 seconds: "Engineering with taste, proven in public."
- Belief ladder: these are real systems → they're maintained in public → that takes strong engineering and design judgment → this is worth exploring further
- Proof on hand: shipped projects, demos, and maintained repositories already linked on the site

## Brand Personality

Precise, opinionated, curious. The emotional goal is confident curiosity — a visitor should feel invited to dig deeper, not sold to. Reference point: Panic's distinctive developer personality, backed by real craft rather than performance.

## Anti-references

Generic résumé templates. SaaS landing-page polish. Terminal cosplay. Editorial affectation.

## Design Principles

- Let the work speak: proof (shipped projects, maintained repos) carries more weight than any claim in copy.
- Depth over decoration: precision and craft over trend-chasing visual flourish.
- Confident, not performative: opinionated voice without cosplay or affectation.
- Public credibility as the throughline: every path should reinforce that this is real, maintained engineering.
